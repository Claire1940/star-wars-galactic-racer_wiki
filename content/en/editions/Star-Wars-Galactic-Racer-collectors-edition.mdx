<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Exclusive Vehicles and Customization Options">

The beating heart of Star Wars: Galactic Racer lies in its diverse repulsorcraft classes. Players are not locked into a single style of play; instead, they must master different chassis types to conquer the distinct planetary hazards of the Outer Rim. The game features four core vehicle classes in the base version, with the Deluxe and Collector's editions adding a fifth exclusive manufacturer set: the Kor Sarun line.

Customization runs deep in this run-based structure. In the paddock, players can interact with rival racers, buy specialized parts from local vendors using earned Galactic Credits, and tune their speeders between grueling tournament legs. Upgrades can be applied to core systems such as the Afterburner, Battery, Cornering, and Ramjet, allowing you to tailor your vehicle to your personal driving style.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Profile | Core Trait | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Landspeeder | Weighty, drift-heavy | Drift | Learning lines, heavy contact |
| Speeder Bike | High straight-line speed | Kinetic Burst | Fast sections, risky overtakes |
| Skim Speeder | Hard banking turns | Knife Edge | Technical handling, sharp corners |
| Podracer | Extreme high-speed | Podracing | High-speed events, Tatooine tracks |
| Kor Sarun Deluxe | Balanced premium builds | Custom Arcade Set | Exclusive trials, versatile stats |

<Callout type="tip" title="Tuning Strategy">
When starting your campaign, focus your early Galactic Credits on Cornering and Afterburner upgrades. Pure straight-line speed is highly punishing on narrow, hazard-filled tracks like the canyons of Tatooine.
</Callout>
