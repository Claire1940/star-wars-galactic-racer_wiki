<Section icon={Target} title="Gameplay Modes, Customization, and Beginner Flow">

At its core, the game merges high-speed racing with a runs-based roguelite campaign structure. Players must manage resources, upgrade their vehicles, and survive escalating challenges to maintain their standing in the Galactic League.

### Core Gameplay Mechanics
- **Runs-Based Galactic Tour**: The single-player campaign challenges you to complete a three-act tour with branching paths. Every choice leads to different race types, rewards, and environmental hazards.
- **Customization & Upgrades**: Between races, players retreat to the paddock. Here, you can talk to fellow racers, purchase performance parts from vendors using Galactic Credits, and upgrade core vehicle attributes like Afterburner, Battery, Cornering, and Ramjet.
- **High-Risk Mechanics**: Competing in a tour requires a League Entry Token. Crashing out too many times or failing to place in the top three can cost you your token, ending your run. However, some progress, such as accumulated Galactic Credits, carries over to your next attempt.

| Gameplay Mode | Player Count | Focus & Progression |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Campaign** | 1 Player | Story-driven runs, branching paths, permanent unlocks |
| **Scenario** | 1 Player | Challenge-focused standalone race formats |
| **Arcade** | 1 Player | Curated events showcasing specific vehicles and locations |
| **PvP Grudge Match** | 2-12 Players | Online competitive multiplayer with high-risk ranking |
