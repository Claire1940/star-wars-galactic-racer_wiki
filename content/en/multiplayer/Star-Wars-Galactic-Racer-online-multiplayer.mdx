<Section icon={Swords} title="Vehicles, Builds, and Customization">

Piloting in the Outer Rim requires a deep understanding of your machine. The game features four primary vehicle classes, each offering a distinct handling profile, specialized traits, and unique upgrade paths. Your choice of chassis completely changes how you approach corners, manage straightaways, and interact with other racers during online matches.

To succeed in competitive lobbies, you must customize your build to counter your opponents' strategies. Visual customization, such as pre-order liveries and player banners, allows you to stand out in the starting grid.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Profile | Unique Trait | Primary Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landspeeder** | Weighty, drift-heavy handling | **Drift** | Surviving contact-heavy races and learning tight corners |
| **Speeder Bike** | Agile, high straight-line speed | **Kinetic Burst** | Overtaking on long straightaways; highly fragile |
| **Skim Speeder** | Technical, sharp banking turns | **Knife Edge** | High-precision driving on winding, narrow tracks |
| **Podracer** | Extreme speeds, fragile build | **Podracing** | High-speed showcase events and open-arena circuits |

To prepare your vehicle for high-level online competition, follow this upgrade and tuning progression path:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Base Chassis">
    Choose between a Landspeeder, Speeder Bike, or Skim Speeder based on your preferred handling style. Beginners should start with the Landspeeder for its forgiving durability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire Upgrade Parts">
    Earn Galactic Credits by completing campaign runs or placing high in online PvP matches. Use these credits to purchase high-quality components from paddock vendors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Balance Your Stats">
    Tune your vehicle's engine, battery, and cooling systems. Ensure your Afterburner and Ramjet upgrades do not exceed your cooling capacity, or you risk overheating mid-race.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Visual Customization">
    Equip unlocked liveries, such as the Naboo N-1 starfighter-inspired Deluxe livery, and set your custom Player Banner to establish your identity in online lobbies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Test in Arcade Mode">
    Before risking your League Entry Tokens in high-stakes PvP, test your new build in a solo Arcade time trial to fine-tune your drift angles and boost timing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Upgrades Matter">
While cosmetic items carry over freely, performance-enhancing parts must be carefully balanced. Upgrading your cornering stability is often more valuable than pure top speed on technical tracks like Ando Prime.
</Callout>
