<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Gameplay and Progression Guide">

The heart of **Star Wars Galactic Racer 2026** lies in its **Galactic Tour** mode. This is not a linear series of races; instead, it is a runs-based campaign where every choice matters. You must navigate a branching map of events, choosing between high-risk qualifiers, resource-heavy pit stops, and dangerous rival encounters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Entry Point">
    Start a new Galactic Tour by spending a **League Entry Token**. Choose your starting chassis based on the initial planet's terrain (e.g., opting for a Skim Speeder on the icy tracks of Ando Prime).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Navigate the Branching Map">
    The tour map features various nodes. Some offer higher **Galactic Credits**, while others provide rare engine parts or tools. Plan your route to ensure you have enough upgrades before the Act Finale.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Heat Cycle">
    During races, use your **Afterburner** for consistent speed. Only trigger the **Ramjet** for overtakes or takedowns, as it rapidly builds heat. Overheating disables your engines temporarily, often leading to a crash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade at the Paddock">
    Between races, visit the Paddock to spend your earned credits. Focus on **Battery** and **Cornering** early on to improve survival, then move into **Kinetic Burst** or **Drift** traits for performance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Survival Strategy">
In the runs-based format, finishing in the top three is more important than a flashy first-place finish if it means destroying your vehicle. If you crash out too many times, your League Entry Token is revoked, and the tour ends.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Vehicle Classes and Customization">

There are four main classes of repulsorcraft in **Star Wars Galactic Racer 2026**. Each class has a specific "Trait" that defines how it handles corners and combat. Customization allows you to tune these traits further using parts found during your campaign runs.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Style | Unique Trait | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landspeeder** | Weighty / Stable | **Drift** | Contact-heavy races |
| **Speeder Bike** | Agile / Fragile | **Kinetic Burst** | Straight-line speed |
| **Skim Speeder** | Technical / Sharp | **Knife Edge** | Tight, banking turns |
| **Podracer** | Extreme / Dangerous | **Podracing** | High-speed open tracks |

Visual customization is also a major component. Players can unlock liveries, player banners, and unique insignia. The Deluxe Edition, for example, includes a livery inspired by the classic **Naboo N-1 Starfighter**, featuring the iconic yellow and chrome aesthetic.

<Callout type="info" title="Customization Tip">
Performance parts like **Afterburners** and **Batteries** are often specific to your chassis class. Ensure you are buying parts that complement your vehicle's natural trait, such as improving "Knife Edge" responsiveness for Skim Speeders.
</Callout>
