<Section icon={Swords} title="Vehicle Classes and Customization Mechanics">

The heart of the gameplay lies in its diverse vehicle roster. Unlike previous Star Wars racing games that focused solely on podracers, this title introduces multiple classes of repulsorcraft, each requiring a different approach to cornering and combat.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Profile | Known Trait | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landspeeder** | Weighty, Drifting focus | **Drift** | Contact-heavy races and narrow city tracks |
| **Speeder Bike** | High straight-line speed | **Kinetic Burst** | Overtaking on long straights |
| **Skim Speeder** | Technical banking | **Knife Edge** | Sharp turns and mountain circuits |
| **Podracer** | Extreme speed, Fragile | **Podracing** | Iconic high-stakes arena events |

Customization goes beyond just aesthetics. Players can visit the paddock between races to talk to vendors and spend **Galactic Credits** on performance parts. These upgrades are vital for surviving the escalating difficulty of the Galactic League.

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Customization Tip">
Prioritize **Afterburner** and **Battery** upgrades early in your campaign. Having a larger boost reservoir is often more valuable than raw top speed when trying to escape a pack of aggressive rivals.
</Callout>

**Key Performance Upgrades:**
- **Afterburner**: Increases the duration and power of your standard boost.
- **Ramjet**: High-risk, high-reward burst that can cause overheating if used excessively.
- **Chassis Reinforcement**: Reduces the damage taken during shunts and takedowns.
- **Cooling Systems**: Speeds up the recovery time after a Ramjet burst or engine strain.

<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner's Guide to the Galactic Tour">

The single-player campaign is structured as a **runs-based roguelite**. This means that while individual race progress might reset if you crash out too many times, your overall progression and certain unlocks will carry over.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starter Chassis">
    Begin your journey by choosing a base vehicle. The **Landspeeder** is highly recommended for beginners due to its durability and forgiving drift mechanics.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage Your League Entry Tokens">
    Entering a tour requires a Token. If you fail to place in the top three or destroy your vehicle, you lose the Token and must start a new attempt.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Launch Boost">
    Timing your accelerator with the final countdown light provides a significant head start. Combine this with a shield input for a "Perfect Start."
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Rival Takedowns">
    Aggression is rewarded. Use shunts to push rivals into hazards. A successful takedown restores a portion of your boost meter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Overheating Risk">
The **Ramjet** boost is powerful enough to secure a victory, but using it for more than three seconds will cause an engine stall. Use it only on the final stretch of a race.
</Callout>

</Section>
