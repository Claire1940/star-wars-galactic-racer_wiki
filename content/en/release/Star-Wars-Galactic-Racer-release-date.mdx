<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beginner's Guide to Galactic Racing">

Succeeding in the Galactic League requires more than just holding down the accelerator. The physics engine demands careful management of momentum, heat, and defensive shielding. Follow this step-by-step progression guide to survive your first few runs in the Outer Rim.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starting Chassis">
    Before entering a run, choose a chassis that matches your control preferences. The Landspeeder offers a weightier feel and a strong focus on drifting, making it ideal for beginners learning the physics of tight corners.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Launch Boost">
    Timed starts are critical. Press and hold your accelerator exactly as the countdown ends, while simultaneously executing the engine-and-shield input sequence. This grants a massive launch boost without overheating your core.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Heat and Boost Systems">
    Your repulsorcraft features two distinct boost mechanics. Use the standard Afterburner for repeatable speed bursts. Reserve the high-risk Ramjet boost for straightaways or aggressive overtaking, as it generates massive heat that can damage your hull if overused.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Defensive Shunts">
    Contact is inevitable. Use physical shunts to push rival racers into track hazards or walls. Securing clean takedowns clears your racing line and boosts your reputation, but avoid taking excessive counter-damage that could end your run prematurely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Invest in Paddock Upgrades">
    Between races, spend your hard-earned Galactic Credits at paddock vendors. Prioritize Cornering and Afterburner efficiency early on. Raw top speed is useless if you cannot navigate tight canyon turns without crashing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Permadeath and Run Failure">
Failing to place in the top three during campaign events will eventually revoke your League Entry Token. While you will lose your current run upgrades, earned Galactic Credits and permanent cosmetic unlocks carry forward to your next attempt.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Vehicles, Customization, and Racing Worlds">

The Galactic League spans several treacherous planets across the Outer Rim, each offering unique environmental hazards, track layouts, and terrain types. Success depends on choosing the right vehicle class and tuning it to match the planet's specific challenges.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Profile | Known Trait | Best Suited For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landspeeder** | Heavy, drift-focused | Drift | Contact-heavy races, tight city circuits |
| **Speeder Bike** | Agile, high straight-line speed | Kinetic Burst | Long straights, quick overtaking maneuvers |
| **Skim Speeder** | Technical, banking-reliant | Knife Edge | Sharp, continuous turns and narrow paths |
| **Podracer** | Ultra-high speed, fragile | Podracing | Open desert tracks, maximum speed trials |

Each planet in the circuit introduces distinct track conditions that will test your reflexes and vehicle setup:
- **Tatooine**: Home of the iconic Mos Espa Circuit. Expect dusty conditions, narrow canyon shortcuts, and high-speed straightaways.
- **Ando Prime**: A frozen wasteland where slippery ice surfaces drastically reduce your traction, forcing a heavy reliance on drift control.
- **Lantaana**: A dense jungle world featuring lush vegetation, blind corners, and sudden elevation changes.
- **Sentinel I**: A hostile volcanic environment where tracks run alongside active lava flows, creating extreme overheating risks.
- **Jakku**: A desert scrapyard filled with massive ship wreckage, tight debris tunnels, and highly unpredictable terrain.
