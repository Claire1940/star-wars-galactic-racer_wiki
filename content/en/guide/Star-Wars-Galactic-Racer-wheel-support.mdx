<Section icon={Swords} title="How Repulsorcraft Physics Affect Wheel Gameplay">

Unlike traditional asphalt racing games where vehicles maintain constant contact with the ground, repulsorcraft in this game operate on hover physics. This introduces unique handling traits like sliding, vertical banking, and kinetic bursts that behave differently under steering wheel inputs.

| Vehicle Class | Handling Identity | Control Mechanics | Ideal Input Settings |
| Landspeeder | Weightier drifting | Drift mechanics, heavy chassis | High steering sensitivity |
| Speeder Bike | Straight-line speed | Kinetic Burst, rapid correction | Low deadzone for straight lines |
| Skim Speeder | Hard banking turns | Knife Edge, technical banking | High analog stick responsiveness |
| Podracer | Extreme high-speed | Dual-engine management, hazards | Maximum precision steering |

Each vehicle class requires rapid, twitch-based corrections to avoid crashing into track hazards. On narrow tracks like the Mos Espa Circuit or the icy canyons of Ando Prime, the physical rotation limits of a steering wheel (typically 270 to 900 degrees) can make recovering from a bad slide slower than using an analog thumbstick.

  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Analog Deadzones">
