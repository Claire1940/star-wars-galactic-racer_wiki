<Section icon={Rocket} title="Vehicle Classes and Physics Implementation">

A core component of the **Star Wars Galactic Racer engine** is its ability to simulate four distinct classes of vehicles, each with radically different handling profiles. The engine must simultaneously calculate the erratic, high-tension physics of a Podracer's energy binders while managing the sleek, low-altitude gliding of the new Skimspeeder class.

The engine's physics engine is designed to eliminate the concept of a "single ideal racing line." This means the environment is fully interactive, and the "best" path changes based on the vehicle class and current speed.

<Section icon={Settings} title="Vehicle Building and Customization Systems">

One of the most touted features of the **Star Wars Galactic Racer engine** is the comprehensive vehicle building system. This isn't just cosmetic; every part added to a vehicle changes its center of mass, top speed, and cooling efficiency. The engine uses a modular attachment system that allows players to mix and match components from different eras, though using parts from racers like Sebulba or Ben Quadinaros requires specific legacy unlocks.

**Core Building Components:**
- **Engines**: Determines base speed and acceleration.
- **Cooling Units**: Prevents overheating during extended "boost" periods.
- **Repulsorlift Disks**: Affects how high the vehicle floats and its stability over rough terrain.
- **Cockpit/Chassis**: Determines the hit points and character visibility.

<Section icon={Users} title="Multiplayer Architecture and Ranking">

The **Star Wars Galactic Racer engine** includes a robust netcode designed to support 12-player races without "ghosting" or synchronization issues common in high-speed racers. By using a hybrid client-side prediction model, the engine ensures that collisions feel impactful and fair, which is vital for a game developed by the *Burnout* veterans.

**Multiplayer Features:**
- **12-Player Races**: Full grid support for all vehicle classes.
- **Ranking System**: A persistent competitive ladder that tracks skill across different tracks.
- **Arcade Mode**: Quick-play races with specific objectives like "Time Trials" or "Elimination."
- **Social Hubs**: Areas where players can show off their custom-built vehicles before a race.
