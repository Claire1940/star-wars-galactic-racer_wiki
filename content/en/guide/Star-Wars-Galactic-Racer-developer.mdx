<Section icon={Trophy} title="Industry Pedigree and Racing Heritage">

The foundation of Fuse Games is rooted in some of the most celebrated racing franchises in video game history. The studio was founded by senior developers from Criterion Games, the legendary development house behind the *Need for Speed* and *Burnout* series. This heritage brings a wealth of knowledge in high-speed mechanics, vehicular physics, and satisfying arcade gameplay loops to the project.

Founder Matt Webster has a long history of working on high-profile intellectual properties. During his tenure at Criterion, Webster collaborated with DICE on the development of *Star Wars Battlefront* (2015) and *Star Wars Battlefront II* (2017). This unique combination of racing game expertise and direct experience with the sci-fi franchise makes the studio uniquely qualified to handle this project.

<Section icon={Map} title="Game Design Philosophy and Features">

Fuse Games is designing the title as a "racing adventure" rather than a traditional simulator. The game features a single-player campaign that includes off-track narrative elements where players control their character directly, bridging the gap between racing and adventure games. For competitive players, the game will support an online multiplayer mode hosting up to 12 players, complete with a structured ranking system.

The tracks themselves are built around the concept of freedom. The developers have stated that there is no single ideal racing line on the tracks. Instead, players must adapt to dynamic environments, choose their own paths, and utilize the unique handling characteristics of different vehicle classes. The game features iconic planets alongside new locations, and introduces a brand-new vehicle class to the universe: the skimspeeder.

<Callout type="warning" title="Track Design Tip">
Because there is no single ideal racing line, exploring alternative pathways and shortcuts on each planet is key to securing victory in competitive matches.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Development Timeline and Launch Details">

The journey of the game began with the founding of Fuse Games in 2023. After two years of quiet development, the project was officially unveiled to the public in December 2025 with a cinematic trailer at The Game Awards. The studio followed this up with a gameplay trailer in February 2026, showcasing the first look at the racing adventure mechanics, vehicle building, and planetary tracks.

The title is scheduled for a global release on October 6, 2026. It will launch on PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and Windows PC. This release marks the fourth dedicated racing game in the franchise's history, following in the footsteps of *Star Wars Episode I: Racer* (1999), *Star Wars: Super Bombad Racing* (2001), and *Star Wars Racer Revenge* (2002).
