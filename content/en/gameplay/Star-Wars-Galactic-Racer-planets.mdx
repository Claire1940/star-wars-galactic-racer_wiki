### Jakku
Jakku is an unsanctioned, high-risk racing location characterized by its dusty desert terrain and massive fields of fallen military starships. Racers must navigate through the decaying hulls of ancient cruisers, making sharp turns around jagged metal structures. The air is thick with dust, occasionally reducing visibility during intense pack racing.

### Tatooine
The spiritual home of podracing, Tatooine features wide-open sand flats contrasted with incredibly narrow, winding rocky canyons. The famous Mos Espa Circuit and surrounding canyon routes demand extreme speed but punish minor steering errors with devastating wall collisions. Sand dunes can launch vehicles into unexpected jumps, requiring careful pitch control.

### Ando Prime
Ando Prime introduces a severe low-friction handling model. The frozen tracks and slippery ice sheets make standard braking highly ineffective. Racers must rely on heavy banking and controlled drifts to maintain speed through sweeping glacial turns. The biting cold helps mitigate engine overheating, allowing for slightly longer boost windows.

### Lantaana
Lantaana is a lush, tropical jungle world that hosts some of the most technical tracks in the Galactic League. The circuits wind through massive root systems and dense foliage, creating numerous blind corners. Finding the optimal racing line here is difficult, as the natural boundaries of the track are highly irregular and unforgiving to wide drifts.

### Sentinel I
Sentinel I is a volcanic nightmare where the track itself is a hazard. Active magma streams cross the racing surface, requiring precise jump timing or rapid lane changes. The ambient temperature is exceptionally high, causing vehicle engines to overheat much faster when using Afterburners or Ramjets.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Vehicle Adaptation and Chassis Selection">

Choosing the correct vehicle chassis is just as important as knowing the track layout. Repulsorcraft in the Galactic League are divided into distinct classes, each possessing unique handling profiles, weight characteristics, and specialized traits. Matching your chassis to the planetary terrain can mean the difference between a podium finish and a catastrophic crash.

### Chassis Classes and Environmental Compatibility
The three core chassis types—Landspeeders, Speeder Bikes, and Skim Speeders—perform differently depending on the surface conditions of the active planet. Additionally, high-speed Podracers offer unparalleled straight-line performance but require expert reflexes to control on narrow, winding tracks.

- **Landspeeders**: Weightier handling with a strong drifting identity. These vehicles are highly durable and excel at surviving the contact-heavy, wreckage-filled tracks of Jakku and the rocky canyons of Tatooine.
- **Speeder Bikes**: Extremely fast in straight lines but highly unstable around tight corners. Their Kinetic Burst ability makes them ideal for the wide-open flats of Tatooine, though they struggle significantly on the winding jungle tracks of Lantaana.
- **Skim Speeders**: Engineered for hard banking turns using their unique Knife Edge trait. These highly technical craft are perfect for navigating the dense, twisting forest paths of Lantaana and the sharp corners of Sentinel I.
- **Podracers**: The ultimate speed machines, featuring dual engines linked by energy binders. They dominate high-speed arena events like the Derven Acos Arena but are fragile and highly susceptible to wall collisions.

| Vehicle Class | Primary Trait | Best Planet | Handling Profile | Durability |
|------|------|------|------|------|
| **Landspeeder** | Drift | Jakku | Heavy, drift-focused | High |
| **Speeder Bike** | Kinetic Burst | Tatooine | Light, straight-line speed | Medium |
| **Skim Speeder** | Knife Edge | Lantaana | Agile, high banking | Medium |
| **Podracer** | Podracing | Derven Acos | Extreme speed, low traction | Low |

<Callout type="warning" title="Collision Warning">
Lightweight vehicles like Speeder Bikes can easily be shunted off the track by heavier Landspeeders. If you are racing on narrow tracks like Lantaana or Sentinel I, avoid trading paint with heavier opponents unless you have a clear tactical advantage.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Campaign Progression Strategy">

Succeeding in the runs-based Galactic Tour campaign requires a methodical approach to choosing your routes, managing your resources, and upgrading your repulsorcraft between races. Use this systematic progression guide to navigate your way from an Outer Rim rookie to a Galactic League champion.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starting Chassis">
    Begin your campaign by choosing a Landspeeder. Its high durability and forgiving drift mechanics make it the safest choice for learning the basic physics, collision mechanics, and boost timing on early Jakku circuits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire League Entry Tokens">
    Compete in qualifying races and lower-tier events to build up your bank of Galactic Credits and secure League Entry Tokens. Treat these tokens as your life pool; losing them all ends your current campaign run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Upgrades for the Next Planet">
    Before entering a new planetary circuit, visit the paddock. Check the environmental profile of the upcoming track and spend your Galactic Credits on targeted parts, such as improved cooling manifolds for Sentinel I or advanced drift stabilizers for Ando Prime.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Risk-Based Boosting">
    During races, manage your Afterburner and Ramjet systems carefully. Use standard Afterburner boosts on straightaways, and save high-risk Ramjet bursts exclusively for executing takedowns on rivals or recovering from cornering mistakes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Confront Rival Pilots">
    As you reach the end of a planetary bracket, you will face rival pilots like Kestar Bool in high-stakes showdowns. Study their racing lines, capitalize on their cornering errors, and use aggressive shunts to force them into track hazards.
  </Step>
</Steps>
