<Section icon={Target} title="Tactical Combat Maneuvers: Step-by-Step">

Executing a successful takedown requires more than just raw speed. You must master the timing of your lateral movements and understand the physics of repulsorcraft. The following steps outline the most effective way to eliminate a rival in **Star Wars Galactic Racer combat**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Approach and Draft">
    Position yourself directly behind the target to enter their slipstream. This grants a speed boost and hides your intentions. Wait for the target to approach a corner or a narrow section of the track.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Lateral Alignment">
    Move alongside the opponent. In *Star Wars: Galactic Racer*, you want to be slightly ahead of their center of mass. This allows you to hook their front end during a shunt, causing them to lose control more easily.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Executing the Shunt">
    Initiate a hard lateral thrust towards the opponent. If timed correctly, your repulsors will "lock" momentarily with theirs, transferring your momentum and sending them reeling toward the track boundary.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Takedown Confirmation">
    Once the opponent is off-balance, follow up with a second slam to ensure they hit a hard object or fall into a hazard. A successful "Takedown" will instantly refill your boost meter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Maneuver | Risk Level | Reward | Best Use Case |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Side Shunt** | Medium | High Boost | Crowded straights |
| **Tail Tap** | Low | Disrupts Steering | Before sharp turns |
| **Grind Out** | High | Guaranteed Takedown | Long walls or guardrails |
| **Air Slam** | Very High | Massive Reputation | During jumps or vertical drops |

<Callout type="info" title="Boost Management">
Combat maneuvers consume a small portion of your energy, but a successful takedown provides a full meter. Managing this "Economy of Violence" is key to maintaining top speed throughout the race.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Planetary Hazards and Environmental Combat">

The tracks in the Galactic League are as dangerous as the pilots themselves. From the frozen wastes of Ando Prime to the searing sands of Tatooine, the environment plays a massive role in **Star Wars Galactic Racer combat**. Skilled pilots don't just fight other racers; they use the planet itself as a weapon.

| Planet | Hazard Type | Combat Impact | Tactical Advice |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Tatooine** | Sand Drifts | Reduces traction significantly | Shunt rivals into deep sand to stall them |
| **Ando Prime** | Ice Sheets | Causes uncontrollable sliding | Use heavy slams to spin rivals on the ice |
| **Lantaana** | Corrosive Mist | Obscures vision and damages hull | Force rivals into the mist to drain their shields |
| **Sentinel I** | Gravity Wells | Pulls craft toward the center | Bait rivals into the well and boost away |
| **Jakku** | Scavenger Debris | Large physical obstacles | Use "Slam" to push rivals into downed Star Destroyers |

On Ando Prime, for example, the lack of friction means that a single well-placed shunt can send an opponent sliding for hundreds of meters. Mastering the "Ice Slide" allows you to maintain combat effectiveness even when your vehicle is technically out of control. Meanwhile, on Tatooine, the "Boonta Eve Classic" circuits feature narrow canyons where a single crash can create a bottleneck, allowing you to take down multiple rivals at once.

<Callout type="success" title="Hazard Mastery">
Watch for the "Hazard Warning" icons on your HUD. These indicate upcoming environmental traps that can be triggered by physical contact or proximity. Triggering these at the right moment can wipe out an entire pack of pursuers.
