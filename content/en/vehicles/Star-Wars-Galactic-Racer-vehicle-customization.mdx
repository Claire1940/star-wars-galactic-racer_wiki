To survive the lawless tracks of the Outer Rim and conquer the Galactic League, understanding the foundation of **Star Wars Galactic Racer vehicle customization** is absolutely essential. Every build begins with selecting a base chassis. Your choice of chassis dictates your handling profile, drifting capabilities, and how your subsequent stat upgrades behave on the track. 

The game features four distinct repulsorcraft classes, each catering to a different style of driving. Whether you prefer the weightier, drift-heavy mechanics of a landspeeder or the sheer, volatile velocity of a podracer, your base vehicle choice sets the limits of your performance potential. 

<Callout type="success" title="Chassis Selection Strategy">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Performance Parts and Stat Upgrades">

Performance customization in Star Wars: Galactic Racer is deep and granular. Between races in the campaign, you will spend your hard-earned Galactic Credits in the paddock to buy parts from local vendors. These parts directly influence five core performance categories. Balancing these stats is crucial because over-allocating into raw speed without investing in handling or cooling is a quick way to end your tournament run in a spectacular crash.

Upgrades are applied to specific subsystems of your repulsorcraft. For example, upgrading your Battery allows you to sustain longer boosts, while upgrading your Cornering components increases your lateral grip, preventing you from sliding into track hazards.

<Callout type="warning" title="Overheating and Boost Management">
While the Ramjet boost provides unmatched acceleration for overtaking rivals, it generates extreme heat. Upgrading your cooling systems alongside your boost components is vital to prevent your engines from overheating and forcing a catastrophic system failure.
</Callout>

| Upgrade Category | Primary Effect | Risk/Benefit | Key Tunable Parts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Afterburner | Repeatable, sustained speed boosts | Consumes battery power steadily | Thrust injectors, fuel lines |
| Battery | Increases energy capacity for boosts | Adds minor weight to the chassis | Ion cells, power couplers |
| Cornering | Enhances lateral grip and banking angle | Reduces drift slide distance slightly | Repulsor coils, stabilizing fins |
| Ramjet | High-risk, explosive burst of speed | High risk of engine overheating | Combustion chambers, intake manifolds |
| Chassis Traits | Enhances class-specific abilities | Requires specific chassis compatibility | Drift stabilizers, kinetic dampeners |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Customizing Your First Speeder">

Building your first competitive repulsorcraft can feel overwhelming with so many parts and stats to manage. Follow this step-by-step progression route to build a balanced, reliable vehicle capable of surviving early Galactic League qualifiers.

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Tip">
During the early stages of the campaign, focus your limited Galactic Credits on handling and cooling rather than raw speed. A stable vehicle that finishes races safely yields more consistent rewards than a volatile speed build that frequently crashes out.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Base Chassis Class">
    Visit the paddock garage and select your starting chassis. For your first campaign run, choose the Landspeeder. Its superior stability and high shield capacity provide a safe environment to learn drifting mechanics and track layouts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire Galactic Credits in Early Races">
    Compete in early qualifier events on Jakku or Tatooine. Focus on clean racing lines and avoid excessive contact to maximize your credit payout. Do not worry about aggressive tuning until you have accumulated a comfortable credit reserve.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Cornering and Afterburner Upgrades">
    Once you have credits, visit the paddock vendor. Purchase Tier 1 Cornering upgrades to improve your vehicle's responsive handling. Follow up with Afterburner upgrades to give yourself a reliable, controllable speed boost on straight sections.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Tune Your Class-Specific Chassis Traits">
    Install class-specific parts that enhance your vehicle's natural strengths. For the Landspeeder, invest in Drift Stabilizers to prolong your controlled slides and generate boost energy more efficiently during tight turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Apply Visual Customizations and Liveries">
    Open the visual customization menu to apply earned liveries and update your Player Banner. Personalize your repulsorcraft to establish your identity in the Galactic League before taking your customized build into online multiplayer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Customization Strategy for Tournament Runs">

In the runs-based Galactic Tour campaign, vehicle customization is a dynamic process of risk and reward. You do not keep all of your upgrades permanently; instead, you must manage your build dynamically across a series of branching events. To enter these high-stakes events, you must risk a League Entry Token. Crashing out of a race or failing to secure a top-three finish can cost you your token, ending your current tour and resetting your temporary vehicle upgrades.

Therefore, your customization strategy must adapt to the specific rules of the mode you are playing. Campaign mode demands a balanced, survival-oriented build, while Arcade and PvP modes allow for more specialized, high-risk performance tuning.

<Callout type="success" title="Galactic Credit Preservation">
While some upgrades reset upon tour failure, a portion of your earned Galactic Credits carries forward to your next attempt. Use these retained credits to permanently unlock advanced paddock vendors, giving you access to higher-tier parts earlier in subsequent runs.
</Callout>

| Feature | Campaign Mode | Arcade Mode | PvP Grudge Matches |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upgrade Permanence | Temporary (Resets on tour failure) | Preset based on selected racer | Permanent unlock progression |
| Resource Cost | Spent using earned Galactic Credits | Free (Curated events) | Unlocked via multiplayer rank |
| Risk Factor | High (Can lose League Entry Token) | None (Practice environment) | Reputation stakes and rank points |
| Tuning Flexibility | High (Dynamic part swapping) | Low (Locked to event parameters) | High (Fully customized builds) |
