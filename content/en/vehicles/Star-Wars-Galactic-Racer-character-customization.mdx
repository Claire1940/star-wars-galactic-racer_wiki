This decision was made to support a "stronger narrative focus," according to Matt Webster. Because Shade is a canonical figure, his journey through the Galactic League is a fixed point in the Star Wars timeline.

| Aspect | Customizable? | Reason |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Species** | No | Fixed as Human for narrative consistency |
| **Gender** | No | Shade is a specific male canonical character |
| **Jedi Status** | No | Shade is a racer, not a Force user |
| **Backstory** | No | Tied to the game's specific campaign |
| **Vehicle Choice** | Yes | Extensive pod and speeder customization |

Despite these limitations, the "creative partnership" with Lucasfilm has allowed Fuse Games to innovate within the racing genre. They are not just using the license; they are building new lore that fits seamlessly with the films and series. This includes new designs for racing gear that have never been seen in Star Wars before, balanced against classic looks like the Endor speeder bike outfits.
